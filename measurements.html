Introduction to 2D shapes

  • Dimensions are referred to as measures of size such as length, width, base and height of an object or a shape.
  • Length - Length refers to the longest dimension of an object, measured horizontally or left to right.
  • Base - Refers to both the side and its length (the measurement). Height is usually perpendicular to the base.
  • Height - The measurement of someone or something base to top, measure vertically or down-up.
  • Width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side.
  • 2D shapes are therefore consists of two of these dimensions.

Area

  • A 2 dimensional quantity, measuring the amount of space inside a 2D shape.
  • Measured in unit squared, e.g., cm2, m2, etc.

    • Area of a rectangle

  • A = Length x Width
  • Length being the longer side and Width the shorter side.
  • Calculate the area of a rectangle with a length of 10 cm and With of 6 cm
  • A = L x W
  • A = 10 cm x 6 cm
  • A = 60 cm2

    • Area of a square

  • Since unlike a rectangle, all side of a square are equal.
  • Hence, we can not distiguish the sides in terms of Length and Width,
  • The area of a square is usually calculated using A = side x side or A = side2.
  • Calculate the area of a square with sides of 10 cm.
  • A = s2
  • A = 10 cm2
  • A = 100 cm2

    • Area of a triangle

  • When you cut a rectangle exactly in half along a diagonal line from opposite corners, you get two right-angle triangles with each exactly half of the cut rectangle.
  • Each right-angle triangle therefore has half the area of the rectangle.
  • Therefore, area of a right-angle triangle = (L * W)/2.
  • However, the names Length and Width, will not work well for acute and obtuse because it would be hard to tell which one is length and width.
  • Instead, we choose one of the three sides to be base and the one perpendicular to the base, is height.
  • Hence, formula for Traingles Area is A = (B * H)/2
  • Calculate the area of a right-angle triangle with sides of 10 cm.
  • A = (B * H)/2
  • A = (9m * 4m)/2
  • A = 18m2
  • For more information click the button below:

    • Area of a Trapezium

  • A = ((sum of the parallel lines) * height)/2
  • Calculate the area of a trapezium with opposite sides equal to 10 cm and 15 cm respectively, with a height of 11 cm.
  • A = ((sum of the parallel lines) * height)/2
  • A = ((a + b) * h)/2
  • A = ((10 + 15) * 11cm)/2
  • A = 137.50 cm2

Circles

  • Circles are 2d shape formed when a set of points equidistant from a fixed point is called a circle.
  • We already know that the distance around a 2d shape is called a perimeter. However, in circles, this is referred to as circumference.
  • The distance between any point of the circumference and the center is called the radius.
  • The distance between any two points of the circumference through the center is called the diameter.
  • The diameter is two times the radius.
  • Therefore, diameter = 2* radius and radius = diameter/2.
  • Pi = is the ratio of the circumference and diameter of any given circle, and it is always the same.
  • Therefore,
  • Pi or π = circumference/diameter
  • The value of pi is irrational, so it is usually represented as a two-decimal places number of 3.14.

Introduction to 3D shapes - Cognito

3D shapes, or three-dimensional shapes, have three dimensions: length, width, and height. They occupy space and have volume, distinguishing them from 2D shapes, which only have length and width.

Was this video helpful?

thumb_up thumb_down
×

3D shapes

  • 3D shapes, or three-dimensional shapes, have three dimensions: length, width, and height. They occupy space and have volume, distinguishing them from 2D shapes, which only have length and width.
  • Here are some common 3D shapes:
  • Cube: All faces are squares, and all edges are equal.
  • Cuboid (Rectangular Prism): Faces are rectangles, and opposite faces are equal.
  • Pyramid: A base that is a polygon (commonly a square or triangle) with triangular faces converging to a single point (vertex).
  • Tetrahedron: A pyramid with a triangular base and four triangular faces.
  • Cylinder: Has two parallel circular bases connected by a curved surface.
  • Cone: Has a circular base and a single vertex, with a curved surface connecting them.
  • Sphere: Every point on the surface is equidistant from the center.
  • Hemisphere: A three-dimensional shape that's half of a sphere with one flat, circular side..

Surface Area

The sum of all areas of faces of a polyhedral.

Faces are all the two-dimensional shapes that are found in a 3d shape.

  • Cube
    A cube is made of 6 square face; therefore, the surface area of a cube is a2+ a2 + a2 + a2 + a2 + a2.
    Factor our a2:
    a2(1+1+1+1+1+1+1)
    Therefore, the final equation is a2(6).
  • Cuboid or rectangular prism
    A rectangle prism comprises of 3 different rectangles based on length, Width, or Height, with each rectangle going in pairs.
    The first pair comprises the 2 dimensions of height & length (LH), in the diagram above these, are front and back triangles.
    The second pair is made up of the 2 dimensions of length and width (LW), in the diagram above the top and bottom triangles.
    The third pair is one made up of 2-dimension of height and width (HW), in the diagram above these are right and left triangles.
    Is the sum of all the 3 different triangles multiplied by two, because they all go in pairs.
    SA = 2*LH + 2*LW + 2*HW
    Factor out 2:
    The final equation is then:
    SA = 2(LH + LW + HW)
  • Square pyramid
    Made up 1 square and 4 triangles.
    Therefore, the surface area of the square prism is the sum of the areas of all its triangles plus the area of that one square.
    Area of a square = s2
    Area of a triangle = (b *h)/2 or ½(b * h)
    SA = s2 + (b *h)/2 + (b *h)/2 + (b *h)/2 + (b *h)/2
    SA = s2 + 4(b *h)/2
    SA = s2 + 2(b*h)
    h is the slant height of the pyramid.
  • Triangle pyramid
    The sum of areas of all its 4 triangles.
    The base triangle is usually equilateral. Then you can use the following equation to find its area:
    A = √4/2 * S2
    Surface Area of the Triangle = A + 3/2(b * h)
    A - is the area of the base triangle.
  • Cylinder
    Made up of 2 equal circles and a curved rectangle.
    Therefore, the surface area of a cylinder is the sum of the two circles and the curved rectangle.
    Area of a circle = πr2
    Area of a rectangle = L x W, where Width is equal to the Height of the cylinder and Length is equal to the circumference of the base circle. Circumference of the circle = π2r
    Therefore, the cylinder surface area = πr2 + πr2 + π2r
    SA = 2 πr2+ hπ2r
  • Cone
    Made up of 1 circle and a curved triangle.
    Area of a circle + πrl
    L is the slant height of the cone.
    Therefore, the surface area of a cone = πr2 + πrl
  • Sphere
    Every point on the surface is equidistant from the center.
    Therefore,
    Surface Area of a sphere = 4πr2

Volume equations for each 3d shape

  • Cube:

    Volume = a3
    where a
    a is the length of a side.

  • Cuboid:

    Volume= L * W * H
    Where L is the length
    W is the width,
    and H is the height.

  • Pyramid:

    Volume = 1/3(B * H)
    Where
    B is the area of the base and
    h is the height perpendicular to the base, not the slant height.

  • Cone:

    Volume = 1/3(πr2h)
    Where
    πr2 is the area of the circle of the cone and
    h is the height.

  • Cylinder:

    Volume= πr2* h
    Where
    r πr2 is the area of the bottom circle and
    h is the height.

  • Sphere:

    Volume = 4/3(πr3)
